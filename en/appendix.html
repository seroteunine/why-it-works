Multimeter quick reference

What you're measuring Dial setting Probes Circuit state What you're looking for
Mains voltage (socket) VAC / V~ V/Ω + COM; probes in parallel Live (plugged in) ~230V. Significantly less = supply problem.
DC voltage (PSU output, battery) VDC / V— V/Ω + COM; probes in parallel Live Expected rated voltage ± 10%
Fuse continuity Continuity (buzzer) V/Ω + COM Unpowered Beep = intact. OL/silence = blown.
Heating element resistance Ω (resistance) V/Ω + COM Unpowered, element isolated ~V²/W ohms. OL = open. Near 0 = shorted.
Switch operation Continuity (buzzer) V/Ω + COM Unpowered OFF → OL; ON → beep. Any other result = suspect.
Cable continuity Continuity (buzzer) V/Ω + COM Unpowered Beep = intact. OL = broken conductor.
Motor winding resistance Ω V/Ω + COM; isolated Unpowered Model-specific. OL = open winding. Near 0 = shorted.
Winding-to-frame insulation Ω (highest range) V/Ω + COM Unpowered, motor isolated Should read OL (>1 MΩ). Any low reading = insulation fault.
Capacitance F or µF (cap function) V/Ω + COM; discharged cap Unpowered, discharged Within ±20% of rated value. Lower = degraded.
Diode check Diode (▶|) V/Ω + COM Unpowered, isolated Forward: 0.5–0.7V. Reverse: OL. Other = failed.
Current draw A or mA A socket + COM; probes in series Live, circuit broken to insert meter Compare to P/V from appliance's watt rating.
Safety

Always double-check probe sockets before measuring voltage. Red probe in V/Ω, never in A or mA socket. Never measure resistance or continuity on a live circuit. Discharge capacitors before testing. Use a CAT II 600V or better rated meter for mains work.

Key formulas

Formula What it means Practical use
V = I × R Ohm's law: voltage = current × resistance Calculate expected current (I = V/R) or resistance (R = V/I)
P = V × I Power = voltage × current Find expected current from watt rating: I = P/V
P = V² ÷ R Power from voltage and resistance Calculate expected resistance: R = V²/P
P = I² × R Power (heat) from current and resistance Understand heat in cables: doubles current = 4× heat
Vpeak = VRMS × √2 Peak voltage from RMS (AC mains) 230V RMS → 325V peak. Component ratings must exceed peak.
Rtotal = R₁ + R₂ Series resistance Total resistance increases with each added series element
1/Rtotal = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ Parallel resistance Total resistance decreases; always lower than smallest branch
τ = R × C RC time constant Capacitor discharge time; 5τ ≈ fully discharged

Fault-finding checklist

Before opening anything

  • Safety assessment: damaged cable? Liquid ingress? Burn marks? Class I earth intact?
  • Get the story: sudden failure or gradual? Sound or smell at failure? Anything changed?
  • Identify the class: Class I (metal case, earth wire) or Class II (plastic, double-insulated)?
  • Check the socket: is the mains supply actually present and correct?

Completely dead appliance

  • External fuse (in plug or inline): continuity check
  • Internal fuse: continuity check
  • Main switch: continuity (closed position)
  • Thermal cutoff (TCO): continuity — OL means triggered, find root cause
  • Resettable cutout: press reset button after full cooling
  • Power supply output (if applicable): voltage correct?
  • Load: resistance in expected range?

Partial failure (one function dead)

  • Identify which functions work: this tells you where the common supply chain is intact
  • Identify the branch: trace from the branch point to the dead function
  • Check series elements in that branch: sub-thermostat, sub-TCO, branch switch
  • Check the load in that branch: resistance, continuity
  • Check wiring to branch: cable continuity, connector integrity

Overheating

  • Check airflow: blocked intake, full bag, blocked filter
  • Check thermostat operation: does it cycle? Does it trip at the right temperature?
  • Check bearings: spin shaft by hand — free and smooth?
  • Current draw: compare to rated P/V — higher than rated = extra heat somewhere
  • Check for hotspots: feel (safely) for components hotter than the rest

Tripping circuit breaker / blowing fuse

  • Check resistance L-to-N with appliance unplugged and switch off: should not be near zero
  • Isolate branches: disconnect loads one by one until resistance rises — last disconnected = fault location
  • Check capacitors (if in primary circuit): short circuit?
  • Check motor winding to case: insulation fault?
  • Check switch position: tripping before switch is engaged = fault in always-live section

Before returning to owner

  • Earth continuity (Class I): check plug earth pin to chassis — should be <1 Ω
  • Cable integrity: no damage, correct strain relief at appliance and plug
  • Correct fuse type and rating: matches original specification
  • Functional test: run through full operating cycle
  • No unusual heat, smell, or noise during functional test
  • Note written: what fault was found and what was done

When to stop and refer

SituationReason to stop
Microwave or CRT TV internals Lethal stored voltage in capacitors; specialist procedures required
Gas-connected appliances Gas safety is outside electrical repair scope
Fuse blowing repeatedly with no clear cause Risk of fire if appliance returned without root cause resolved
Winding-to-case insulation fault in motor Risk of electric shock from appliance casing
Damaged or absent earth on Class I appliance Must be repaired before returning — latent lethal hazard
PCB-level fault requiring component-level diagnosis Refer to someone with SMD soldering skills and test equipment
Safety-critical components (RCD, smoke detector, CO detector) No margin for error; replace rather than repair
Repair cost approaching replacement cost Honest assessment: sometimes recycling is the right answer
