Before We Start

What's in here

This book has twelve chapters, in roughly the order they become useful at the bench.

Chapters 1–4 cover the foundation: voltage, current, resistance, circuits, series and parallel connections, and power and heat. Chapters 5–12 move into repair practice: the multimeter, common components, AC and DC, reading diagrams, and fault diagnosis.

If you're new to the theory, read straight through. If you're in the middle of a repair, jump to the chapter that matches the appliance or fault in front of you. Each chapter starts from a repair-cafe situation, then explains the theory you need for that situation.

Four kinds of callout appear throughout:

From the workbench

Practical observations — the kind of thing a more experienced volunteer might mention while standing next to you at the bench.

Safety

Read these carefully. Mains electricity can kill, and some appliances hold lethal charge even after unplugging. These boxes tell you when to stop and why.

The underlying principle

The physics behind what you've just read — not just what happens, but why. Skip them if you want; read them if you're curious.

Try it

Interactive elements you can click or manipulate to see how something behaves. No reading required — just try it and see what happens.

Each chapter ends with five questions. Try them before revealing the answers.

Safety and context

This book is written for the Dutch repair-cafe context: 230V at 50Hz, Schuko plugs, and the kinds of appliances that actually arrive on the table. Safety runs through the whole book. Treat every appliance as live until you've proved otherwise, and stop when you're unsure.

Units follow standard practice: ohms (Ω), amperes (A or mA), volts (V), and watts (W). Formulas are explained in plain language before they appear in symbols. Chapter 1 starts with a kettle.
